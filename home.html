Energy Efficient Appliances in Australia

Welcome to your guide for smart appliance choices that can reduce your energy bills and environmental impact.

Why Choose Energy Efficient Appliances?

Appliances can account for around 30% of home energy use in Australian households. Choosing the most energy-efficient appliances can bring significant savings to your electricity bills while reducing greenhouse gas emissions.

Quick Energy Saving Tips

  • Look for Energy Star Ratings: Higher star ratings mean better energy efficiency
  • Right-size your appliances: Don't buy bigger than you need
  • Switch off at the wall: Reduce standby power consumption
  • Regular maintenance: Clean filters and coils to maintain efficiency

Top Energy Consuming Appliances

Fridges & Freezers: ~10% of household energy bills

Heating & Cooling: ~40% of energy use

Water Heating: ~25% of energy bills

Televisions & Entertainment: ~5% of energy consumption

Washing & Drying: ~8% of energy consumption

Australian Energy Efficiency Programs

The Equipment Energy Efficiency (E3) Program helps Australian consumers choose energy-efficient appliances through mandatory energy rating labels and minimum energy performance standards.

Money-Saving Advice

Investment in energy-efficient appliances may cost more upfront, but the reduced running costs will save money over the appliance's lifetime. Many Australian states also offer rebates for purchasing energy-efficient appliances.
